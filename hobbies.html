Interview with Bill Collier

What inspired you to begin wrestling at 25?

“I was 25 and saw my size as a real advantage. I was working construction and one day thought, ‘Why not try wrestling?’ Six weeks into training, I got thrown into a ring, and I never looked back.”

How did training with Ken “Kato” Downs shape your style?

“Ken showed me the difference between having potential and being a professional. He drilled consistency into me. Wrestling isn’t something you master—it's something you keep learning.”

Which win, ESW, IWC, or Greektown, felt most significant?

“Winning the IWC Heavyweight Championship was big. It was the main event, packed crowd, years of grind paying off. That night validated 15 years of work.”

Describe your AEW Dark Elevation matches vs. Jon Moxley and Adam Page.

“Three nights in a row, tag match, then Mox choked me out, then Hangman caught me with the Lariat. But they both respected me: Mox gave a nod, Page shared a beer. That meant a lot.”

What adjustments did you make to overcome injuries in 2017?

“Wrist and knee issues forced me to smarten up. Less impact, more rehab, more mobility work. I had to evolve my in-ring pacing.”

How do you balance wrestling with family and work?

“I operate heavy machinery full-time, I’m a husband, and I’m a dad. My weekends are packed. I’ve driven to Toronto and back in a day for a match.”

What’s behind your nickname “Big Time”?

“I’m 6'5", 255. I bring a presence, and I show up for big moments. ‘Big Time’ isn’t just the nickname; it’s the standard I set.”

Who in AEW would you most like to face next?

“Wardlow. Or The Butcher & The Blade. I’ve worked with all of them on the indies, they’re tough, and I respect them.”

What’s your training and diet routine like now?

“Core, cardio, ring drills, plus rehab and stretching. Diet is clean, lean meats, veggies. I stay sharp year-round.”

What are your long-term goals in wrestling?

“A contract with AEW is the dream. But more than that? To prove that it’s never too late. That’s what I stand for.”
